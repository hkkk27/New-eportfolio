Digital Literacy

Digital Literacy is a core course offered at FLAME University in the first year of the undergraduate program. This 2-credit course is essential in today’s technological era, equipping students with the knowledge and skills to navigate digital spaces effectively and securely. Through this course, I have gained hands-on experience in various aspects of digital proficiency—from creating an e-portfolio to coding with HTML and CSS. It has enhanced my technical abilities and broadened my understanding of how to leverage technology efficiently. Below, you’ll find a showcase of my work, demonstrating the skills I’ve acquired.

Key Takeaways & Experience

I'm amazed at how much I've grown in such a short time during the Digital Literacy course. This course significantly enhanced my understanding of technology for both personal and professional use. The HTML and CSS tutorials, in particular, transformed my web design skills. Before, I had basic knowledge, but now I can confidently design websites and build my own ePortfolio—which you can see at the top of this section. The journey wasn’t just about learning technical skills. The experience of creating projects, such as the Bucket list and exploring Modern Ai, was both enlightening and practical. I also learned how to navigate the importance of online security and digital footprints, making me aware of the ethical implications of my online actions. One of the most valuable lessons was participating in discussion forums and assignments with peers, which not only helped clarify concepts but also fostered a collaborative learning environment. Most importantly, this course instilled in me the idea that lifelong learning and adaptation are key to becoming truly digitally literate. I'm excited to apply these skills in my academic and professional journey, making this transformation from illiteracy to digital literacy life-changing.

Learning HTML & CSS

When I first dabbled in HTML and CSS, it felt like discovering a new avenue for personal expression. Although I had some basic knowledge of these languages before, this course truly enhanced my skills. I realized that even something as intricate as a webpage could be created by me. HTML provides the structure of a website, while CSS brings it to life by adding colors, fonts, and layouts. It was like assembling a puzzle—once the structure was in place, I could add my personal touch to it.

The process was made much easier thanks to the clear guidance throughout the course. We were walked through the setup and tools needed, which gave me confidence in navigating the coding process. For my first project, I created a personal website using simple HTML tags like <h1>, <p>, and <img>. To make the page unique, I experimented with CSS to customize colors and layouts. It was incredibly rewarding to see my name, picture, and brief introduction neatly displayed on the page. This was more than just a simple project; it marked my first step into web creation.

In addition to my personal website, I also worked on several projects using HTML and CSS. The ePortfolio you can see was made using a template code, but I later designed my own, which you can view at the top of this section. Additionally, if you see the Privacy Policy and Help Pages of my ePortfolio by scrolling down, you’ll notice that these pages were created by me as well. This experience has sparked my enthusiasm for web design and development, and I’m excited to continue enhancing my projects.

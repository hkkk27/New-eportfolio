Digital Literacy

Digital Literacy is a core course offered at FLAME University in the first year of the undergraduate program. This 2-credit course is essential in today’s technological era, equipping students with the knowledge and skills to navigate digital spaces effectively and securely. Through this course, I have gained hands-on experience in various aspects of digital proficiency—from creating an e-portfolio to coding with HTML and CSS. It has enhanced my technical abilities and broadened my understanding of how to leverage technology efficiently. Below, you’ll find a showcase of my work, demonstrating the skills I’ve acquired.

Key Takeaways & Experience

I'm astounded by how much I've grown and evolved in such a short amount of time when I think back on my time in the Digital Literacy course. Each module altered my approach to using technology for both personal and professional goals, in addition to imparting useful skills. The core HTML and CSS tutorials were among the most useful. I had a very basic understanding of web programming prior to this training, but being able to design my own web pages changed everything. When my ePortfolio was finally operational, I was overcome with excitement. It was more than just posting stuff online—it was about presenting my background, abilities, and inventiveness. The adventure story task was especially enjoyable since it allowed me to express myself creatively by fusing technological know-how with storytelling. I was really engrossed in the data analysis and visualization modules. Although I've always been interested in information interpretation, this course really helped me understand how to use Excel in practical ways. Making data visuals like charts and graphs gave me a sense of empowerment, and I had a great time working on the budget tracker project. Seeing how I could use these ideas in practical settings to manage my finances more intelligently and easily was satisfying. Another important lesson was realizing the need for online security and safety. Knowing how to protect personal information has become essential in the modern digital world. I've had to reconsider how I manage my online presence in light of the conversations about digital footprints and the significance of acting morally online. It gives me comfort to know that in this highly linked world, I am now better prepared to participate in safe and responsible ways. The knowledge checks that followed each topic were really helpful in determining what I had learnt. They provided me with the opportunity to clarify my comprehension and pinpoint areas in which I needed to pay closer attention. Another pleasure were the numerous assignments and discussion forums. Participating in these conversations with peers promoted a helpful learning atmosphere. Hearing other people's opinions and exchanging perspectives improved my understanding and made the learning process more engaging. This experience has taught me that developing a mentality that emphasizes lifelong learning and adaptation is just as important to becoming digitally literate as knowing how to use technologies. Speaking of these abilities, I am really looking forward to applying them in my further academic and career practice. I have developed the interest and confidence in proceeding with the subjects that I have passion for, and thus, made me have an eye-opening and quite life-changing journey in changing my illiteracy into digital literacy.

Learning HTML & CSS

When I first dabbled in HTML and CSS, it felt like discovering a new avenue for personal expression. Although I had some basic knowledge of these languages before, this course truly enhanced my skills. I realized that even something as intricate as a webpage could be created by me. HTML provides the structure of a website, while CSS brings it to life by adding colors, fonts, and layouts. It was like assembling a puzzle—once the structure was in place, I could add my personal touch to it.

The process was made much easier thanks to the clear guidance throughout the course. We were walked through the setup and tools needed, which gave me confidence in navigating the coding process. For my first project, I created a personal website using simple HTML tags like <h1>, <p>, and <img>. To make the page unique, I experimented with CSS to customize colors and layouts. It was incredibly rewarding to see my name, picture, and brief introduction neatly displayed on the page. This was more than just a simple project; it marked my first step into web creation.

In addition to my personal website, I also worked on several projects using HTML and CSS. The ePortfolio you can see was made using a template code, but I later designed my own, which you can view at the top of this section. Additionally, if you see the Privacy Policy and Help Pages of my ePortfolio by scrolling down, you’ll notice that these pages were created by me as well. This experience has sparked my enthusiasm for web design and development, and I’m excited to continue enhancing my projects.
